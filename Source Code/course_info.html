Course Information

Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA)

CCNA certification validates your ability to install, configure, operate, and troubleshoot medium-size routed and switched networks.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

The CCNA curriculum in Cisco Networking Academy is designed for those seeking entry-level ICT jobs or preparing for specialized skills. It covers a range of networking topics, providing hands-on experience in installing, configuring, and troubleshooting basic IPv4 and IPv6 networks. The course includes LAN and IP router configuration, security, redundancy, WAN connectivity, OSPF configuration, and device management. Interactive learning tools enhance the experience.

For every student who does well in all three courses, a voucher for a discount will be offered to finish the CCNA career certificate. This is not something that UoM can manage. The Cisco Networking Academy USA is in charge of it.


Course Outline


CCNA: Introduction to Networks
The course "Introduction to Networks" spans over 70 hours and provides an in-depth understanding of the architecture, structure, functions, and components of the Internet and other computer networks. Through this course, students can learn the fundamentals of network operations, how to build basic local area networks (LAN), perform elementary router and switch configurations, and implement Internet Protocol (IP).

CCNA: Switching, Routing, and Wireless Essentials
The course "Switching, Routing, and Wireless Essentials" spans over 70 hours and teaches the fundamental concepts of routers, switches, and wireless local area networks (WLAN) along with security essentials. It covers the architecture, components, and operations of routers and switches in small networks, and provides an insight into configuring and troubleshooting routers and switches for advanced functionality using security best practices. Additionally, the course teaches how to resolve common issues with protocols in both IPv4 and IPv6 networks.

CCNA: Enterprise Networking, Security, and Automation
The course titled "Enterprise Networking, Security, and Automation (70 Hours)" covers the fundamentals of the architecture, components, operations, and security measures involved in scaling up large, complex networks. The course includes an in-depth study of wide area network (WAN) technologies, and focuses on network security concepts. It also introduces the concepts of network virtualization and automation. During the course, students will learn how to configure, troubleshoot, and secure enterprise network devices. Additionally, they will gain an understanding of how application programming interfaces (API) and configuration management tools enable network automation.

CCNA: Switching, Routing, and Wireless Essentials
The course "Switching, Routing, and Wireless Essentials" spans over 70 hours and teaches the fundamental concepts of routers, switches, and wireless local area networks (WLAN) along with security essentials. It covers the architecture, components, and operations of routers and switches in small networks, and provides an insight into configuring and troubleshooting routers and switches for advanced functionality using security best practices. Additionally, the course teaches how to resolve common issues with protocols in both IPv4 and IPv6 networks.


Cisco Certified Network Professional (CCNP)

The new CCNP Enterprise certification program prepares you for today’s professional-level job roles in enterprise networking technologies. CCNP Enterprise now includes automation and programmability to help you scale your networking infrastructure.
One of the industry’s most respected certifications, CCNP validates the core knowledge you need while giving you the flexibility to choose a focus area.
And now every exam in the CCNP Enterprise program earns an individual specialist certification, so you get recognized for your accomplishments along the way.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

CCNP helps students develop the skills needed to succeed in IT-related degree programs and prepare for the Cisco CCNP certification. It provides a theoretically rich, hands-on learning experience covering advanced routing, switching, and troubleshooting skills.
The goals of the CCNP curriculum are as follows:
 Provide an in-depth, theoretical overview of advanced routing, switching, and troubleshooting.
 Equip students with the knowledge and skills necessary to design and support complex enterprise networks.
 Provide an experience-oriented course that employs industry-relevant instructional approaches to prepare students for professional-level jobs in the industry.
 Enable students to gain significant hands-on interaction with IT equipment to prepare them for certification exams and career opportunities.
There are two (02) modules in the CCNP Enterprise certification.

IMPLEMENTING CISCO ENTERPRISE NETWORK CORE TECHNOLOGIES (ENCOR 350-401)
Implementing Cisco Enterprise Network Core Technologies v1.0 (ENCOR 350-401) is a 120-minute exam associated with the CCNP and CCIE Enterprise Certifications. This exam tests a candidate's knowledge of implementing core enterprise network technologies, including dual-stack (IPv4 and IPv6) architecture, virtualization, infrastructure, network assurance, security, and automation. The course, Implementing Cisco Enterprise Network Core Technologies, helps candidates prepare for this exam.
IMPLEMENTING CISCO ENTERPRISE ADVANCED ROUTING AND SERVICES (ENARSI 300-410)
Implementing Cisco Enterprise Advanced Routing and Services v1.0 (ENARSI 300-410) is a 90-minute exam associated with the CCNP Enterprise Certification. This exam certifies a candidate's knowledge for implementation and troubleshooting of advanced routing technologies and services, including Layer 3, VPN services, infrastructure security, infrastructure services, and infrastructure automation. The course, Implementing Cisco Enterprise Advanced Routing and Services, helps candidates prepare for this exam.
